## Mode-by-mode strategy: how to pilot your team correctly

Having the right units is step one. Placing and upgrading them in the right order is what turns a good roster into the **All Star Tower Defense X best team** in practice.

### Story Mode

- Open with farm and one cheap AoE attacker.
- Upgrade economy to your first key threshold, then pivot hard into DPS.
- Add control only when wave pressure starts rising.
- Keep one emergency placement slot unspent for leaks.

### Infinite Mode

- Economy start is still important, but don’t over-greed.
- Transition earlier into scalable DPS.
- Layer control effects instead of firing all actives at once.
- Position utility units to cover multiple lanes/choke points.

### Boss/Event Waves

- Pre-place boss shred units before trigger waves.
- Save active abilities for invulnerability/phase shifts.
- Use control to protect against supporting mobs, not just boss pathing.
- Re-check shield break uptime; that’s where many runs fail.

| Mode | Early Priority | Mid Priority | Late Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Story** | Cheap AoE + farm | Burst DPS | Cleanup consistency |
| **Infinite** | Farm + scalable DPS | Control layering | Buff optimization |
| **Boss/Event** | Utility prep | Boss melt timing | Survival stabilization |

> **💡 Tip:** If your damage looks good on paper but runs still fail, your placement order is likely the issue—not your unit box.
