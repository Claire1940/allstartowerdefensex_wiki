## What Counts as a Limited Unit in 2026?

In All Star Tower Defense X, “limited” does not mean one single thing. Some units are hard-limited to an event window, while others are “soft-limited” because they only appear in rare reruns.

| Limited Type | How You Get It | Availability Pattern | Priority for Most Players |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Event Exclusive** | Event shop, event map milestones, tokens | Short windows (1-4 weeks) | **Very High** |
| **Banner Limited** | Featured summon pool | Rotates in/out | **High** |

- Very hard to replace: units with map-wide utility, special targeting, or unique trait interactions

> **Tip:** Prioritize limited units that do at least two jobs (example: damage + debuff), not just flashy burst damage.

## Team Building Around Limited Units (Without Overdependence)

A good roster uses limited units as force multipliers, not as a single point of failure. Build in layers:

## FAQ

**Q: What are the best All Star Tower Defense X limited units to chase first in 2026?**

A: Start with limited units that provide utility plus damage, especially ones that stay useful across Story, Infinite, and boss-focused content. If your roster lacks debuffs or control, prioritize those before pure DPS options.

**Q: Should I skip a limited banner if I already have one strong carry?**

A: Often, yes. If the banner unit overlaps heavily with your current carry and doesn’t add unique utility, saving for the next rotation can be more efficient for long-term account growth.

**Q: How many gems should I keep in reserve for All Star Tower Defense X limited units?**

A: Keep a fixed reserve based on your average monthly income. Many players use a “never drop below reserve” rule so they can respond to surprise reruns or collab announcements without panic farming.

**Q: Are limited units required to clear hard content?**
