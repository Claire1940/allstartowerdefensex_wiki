## Common mistakes when chasing all star tower defense x best traits

Many players lose progress speed by over-optimizing too early. Avoid these traps:

- **Rerolling off a usable trait too early:** If your tree isn’t built yet, that perfect trait may not outperform a solid fallback.
- **Ignoring economy synergy:** Employment-style setups can create faster total progress than pure DPS during farming loops.
- **Copying Infinite builds into Progressive:** Different wave pacing, different needs.
- **Forgetting map mechanics:** Shield-heavy maps can punish lineups without dedicated answers.
- **Underinvesting in control:** Slow/freeze/stun support can increase “real DPS” more than one extra damage unit.

A clean 2026 progression path is:  
**stability first → farming speed second → trait perfection third**.

**Q: What is the single best trait in all star tower defense x best traits discussions?**

A: Yes, if resources allow. Progressive favors tempo and economy interactions, while Infinite rewards high per-slot scaling and consistent late-wave damage. Splitting builds usually gives better total results in 2026.

**Q: Is Employment worth using over damage traits?**

A: On the right early-game units, yes. Employment-style value can speed up farm curves, letting you max core DPS units sooner. It’s especially useful in repeated trial/challenge farming loops.

**Q: How many times should I target the keyword builds before rerolling again?**

A: Focus on one complete, playable setup first: trait + tree + mode role. Once that unit is fully functional, move to your next core unit. This prevents reroll waste and keeps progression steady.
