## What Counts as a Hybrid Unit in ASTDX?

In practice, a hybrid unit is any unit that reliably contributes in **at least two** categories:

1. Damage output (single-target or AoE)  
2. Survivability (dodge, self-heal, defensive passives)  
3. Team utility (buffs, debuffs, cleanse, aggro control)

A lot of players confuse “high stats” with “hybrid.” A true hybrid can still perform when one lane gets messy, your economy is delayed, or boss pressure forces weird rotations.

| Hybrid Type | Main Job | Secondary Job | Where It Shines |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dodge DPS Hybrid** | Sustained damage | Self-survival via dodge | Boss maps, elite NPC fights |
| **Support DPS Hybrid** | Team buff/debuff | Wave cleanup | Long trials, co-op runs |
| **Bruiser Hybrid** | Frontline soaking | Counter-damage | Mid-game stabilization |
| **Burst Utility Hybrid** | Ability burst | Temporary dodge/defense | Timed mechanics, challenge cards |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Don’t force every team slot into hybrid picks. You still need one or two pure specialists (economy, hard CC, or heavy burst) depending on map rules.

### Dodge-tree hybrid logic (2026 meta)

A major trend in 2026 is dodge stacking on units that already have defensive or counter passives. These units become pseudo-frontliners while still contributing damage. The key is to identify whether dodge helps their *full kit* or blocks part of it.

- Good case: dodge triggers bonus attacks, scaling damage, or bleed/counter effects  
- Neutral case: dodge only improves survival, no extra payoff  
- Risky case: unit requires getting hit sometimes to trigger strongest passive

That last case is where many players overbuild dodge and lower total output.

## Team Building Framework for Hybrid Units

Use this framework to build around **All Star Tower Defense X hybrid units** without weakening your overall comp.

### 1) Assign one “primary pressure lane”
Pick where your best hybrid unit will hold longest. Don’t spread upgrades too early.

### 2) Add one pure utility slot
Hybrid units help, but a dedicated support (buff/debuff/cleanse) improves consistency.

### 3) Include one fail-safe finisher
If your hybrid underperforms due to map modifiers, your finisher handles late bosses.

### 4) Upgrade in role order, not rarity order
Don’t auto-upgrade your rarest unit first. Upgrade whichever unit currently solves the wave.

| Team Slot | Recommended Role | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Slot 1** | Economy or early clear | Stabilizes first waves |
| **Slot 2** | Main hybrid anchor | Mid-game survival + damage |
| **Slot 3** | Utility support | Buff/debuff to amplify anchor |
| **Slot 4** | Secondary hybrid or bruiser | Covers lane leaks |
| **Slot 5** | Boss finisher | Reliable end-wave closeout |
| **Slot 6** | Flex counter-pick | Map-specific answer |

> **💡 Tip:** If your hybrid unit gains value from dodging, position it where enemy contact is frequent enough to trigger passives, but not so overloaded that stun/control mechanics remove uptime.

## FAQ

**Q: Are All Star Tower Defense X hybrid units better than pure DPS units in 2026?**

A: Not by default. Hybrid units are usually better for consistency and flexibility, while pure DPS can still outperform in speed-clear comps. Choose based on map goals and modifiers.

**Q: How many All Star Tower Defense X hybrid units should I run in one team?**

A: Most players get the best results with 2–3 hybrid units, plus 1 utility specialist and 1 finisher. Too many hybrids can cause role overlap and weaker burst.

**Q: Should I always max dodge on hybrid units?**

A: No. Max dodge is strongest when it directly boosts passive value (counterattacks, damage scaling, or uptime). Some units lose damage potential if they rarely get hit.

**Q: What is the fastest way to test a hybrid build?**

A: Run the same unit in three scenarios: a dense wave map, a boss-heavy map, and a modifier challenge. Track clear time, deaths, and passive uptime before committing resources.
