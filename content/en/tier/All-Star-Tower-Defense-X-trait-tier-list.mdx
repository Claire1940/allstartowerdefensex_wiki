## Reroll Priority Path (Beginner to Endgame)

Use this progression route so you don’t overspend on the wrong phase.

### Phase 1: Beginner (Account setup)
- Keep decent B-tier traits on early carries.
- Save premium rerolls for units you’ll still use later.
- Prioritize one stable DPS before chasing perfection.

### Phase 2: Mid-game (Consistency)
- Upgrade your core team to A-tier trait quality.
- Start specializing: one boss killer, one wave clearer, one support anchor.
- Replace temporary economy traits if they slow your clear speed too much.

### Phase 3: Endgame (Optimization)
- Push S-tier traits on low-placement carries first.
- Fine-tune support units with utility-specific rolls.
- Only reroll when the expected gain justifies the cost.

| Phase | Main Goal | Target Trait Quality | Reroll Budget Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Beginner** | Clear story reliably | B to low A | Spend lightly; avoid all-in sessions |
| **Mid-game** | Faster clears and raids | Solid A | Focus on 2–3 core units |
| **Endgame** | Max DPS efficiency | S on key carries | High investment with strict stop rules |

## Common Mistakes That Ruin Trait Value

Even good rolls can underperform if used incorrectly. Avoid these traps:

1. **Over-investing in temporary units**  
   Save your best rerolls for units that stay in your roster for multiple patches.

2. **Ignoring placement limit math**  
   A massive trait on a one-placement carry usually beats moderate traits spread across weak units.

3. **Forcing support traits on damage cores**  
   Team buffs matter, but not at the cost of deleting your boss DPS.

4. **No testing after rerolls**  
   Run practical tests in a repeatable stage. Compare clear time, leaks, and boss wave speed.

5. **Rerolling without a plan**  
   Every spin should have a reason tied to this **All Star Tower Defense X trait tier list** framework.
